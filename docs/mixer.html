🎚️ Welcome to the Mixing Console

Where you become the audio wizard! 🧙‍♂️

🎛️ What's the Mixer?

Think of the mixer as your command center for audio. It's where you take those separated stems (vocals, drums, bass, other) and blend them together into your perfect mix. It's like being a chef, but instead of spices, you're using volume faders and pan knobs!

1

Get Your Stems Ready

Before you can mix, you need stems! Either separate a song (go to the Separate tab!) or manually upload stem files. Once you've got stems, they appear in the "Send to Mixer" section. Just check the boxes next to the stems you want to use and click the button! 📥

2

Meet Your Channel Strips

Each stem gets its own "channel strip" — that's your row of controls for that specific track. From left to right, you'll see:

🎵 Name
☑️ Select
🔇 Mute
🎧 Solo
🎚️ Volume
↔️ Pan
🔇
Mute

Silences that track completely. Useful when you want to hear "everything except the drums" or just need a break from something! 😴

🎧
Solo

This is the "I'm the only important one" button! When you solo a track, ALL other tracks go on vacation (mute). Perfect for focused listening! 🎧

☑️
Select

Check this to include that track in the mix. Uncheck to exclude it. Simple on/off for audio inclusion!

🎚️
Volume

The slider that controls how loud that track is. Slide up = louder, slide down = quieter. Like the volume knob on your remote, but more fun! 📈

↔️
Pan

Decides where the sound comes from in your speakers. Left = left speaker, Right = right speaker, Center = both equally. It's like choosing where to sit in a concert hall! 🎭

3

The Master Section

At the far right of your mixer sits the Master section—your main volume control. This affects the final output of everything combined. Think of it as the main faucet that controls the water to your entire house!

📊 Understanding the Meters

Peak Meter (top): Shows the highest volume moment. If it hits red, you're too loud and things might distort! 🚨

-60dB -12dB 0dB (clip!)

RMS Meter (bottom): Shows the "average" loudness. This is what your ears actually perceive as volume. Think of it like your heart rate— it's the steady rhythm, not the spikes! ❤️

Phase Meter: Tells you about stereo quality. Numbers near +100 mean mono-compatible (safe). Negative numbers? That means phase issues— your speakers might be fighting each other! 🥊

💡 Golden Rules of Mixing:
  • Solo is temporary — Always listen to the full mix, not just soloed tracks!
  • Don't hit red — Keep your peaks below 0dB to avoid distortion
  • Balance > EQ — Try adjusting volume before using fancy filters
  • Pan creates space — Don't stack everything in the center!

5

Spectrum Analyzer

Click the "Spectrum" button to reveal a real-time frequency visualizer! It's like having X-ray vision for sound. Watch the bars dance as music plays— you can literally SEE the bass hitting and the treble singing! 🎆

6

Snapshots & Markers

Two powerful tools for organization:

📸 Snapshots — Save your current mix settings (all volumes, pans, mutes) as a snapshot. Come back later and reload it! It's like saving your game progress.
📍 Markers — Add markers at specific times in your mix. Perfect for noting "drop comes at 2:30" or "change verse here"!
⚠️ Important!

Changes you make in the mixer are temporary! They reset when you refresh. If you want to save your mix settings, use the Snapshot feature. It's like taking a photo of your settings before you close the app! 📸

7

Export Your Masterpiece

Once you're happy with your mix, it's time to share it with the world! Look for the download/export button to save your mixed audio. You can export as WAV (highest quality) or other formats. Go ahead, show the world your creation! 🌟

🎯 Quick Recap:
  • Volume sliders → Control loudness
  • Pan knobs → Control stereo position
  • Mute → Silence a track
  • Solo → Hear only one track
  • Master → Controls final output
  • Snapshots → Save/load mix settings
