## The Basics: Let's Speak the Same Language 🗣️

Before we dive in, let's learn the secret language musicians use. Don't worry—it's easier than learning Klingon.

### 🎼 BPM (Beats Per Minute)

**What it means:** How fast a song marches.

Think of it like your heartbeat. A typical pop song runs around **120 BPM**—that's a moderately excited heartbeat. A slow ballad might be **70 BPM** (relaxed stroll). A fast dance track? **140 BPM** (running late for something!).

**Why you care:** If you're creating a melody, picking the right BPM makes it feel right. Too fast and it sounds frantic. Too slow and it sounds like a sad elephant.

> *Try This:* Tap your foot to your favorite song. That's the BPM. Now you've got it!

### 🎚️ Volume vs. Gain

**Volume** is what you control with the knob on your speaker. **Gain** is the input level going into the system. Think of it like:
- **Gain** = how hard you're blowing into a microphone
- **Volume** = how loud the speaker plays it back

Adjusting gain wrong leads to either quiet/ muddy sound or distorted/clipping sound.

### 🔊 Stems

A **stem** is one piece of a song—like the drums track, or the vocals track. When you "separate" a song, you're turning one audio file into multiple stems.

**Analogy time:** Imagine a pizza. The whole pizza is the song. Each slice? That's a stem. Sometimes you want the whole pizza. Sometimes you just want the pepperoni slice (the vocals) because you're doing a karaoke night. 🍕

### 🎛️ Panning

**Panning** means moving sound left or right in your speakers/headphones.

- **Center (0):** Sound comes equally from both speakers—standard for vocals and most instruments
- **Left (-100):** Sound comes only from the left speaker
- **Right (+100):** Sound comes only from the right speaker

**Why do this?** Imagine a drummer. The hi-hat is usually on the left, the kick drum is center, the snare is center-ish. Panning creates the illusion of space—like you're actually in the room with the band! 🥁

### 🎚️ Mute vs. Solo

| Button | What It Does | When to Use |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **Mute** (speaker icon with X) | Silences that track completely | When you want to hear everything *except* that instrument |
| **Solo** (headphones icon) | Only plays that track, silences all others | When you want to focus on just one instrument |

**Pro-Tip:** Solo is like putting on noise-canceling headphones for one specific instrument so you can check its details.

### 🔊 RMS vs. Peak

Two ways to measure loudness:

- **Peak:** The loudest instantaneous moment—the spike that might clip
- **RMS:** The average sustained loudness—how it actually sounds to your ears

Think of RMS as the "real" volume and Peak as the "hey watch out!" warning. Both meters help you mix properly!

### 🎚️ Modulation

**Modulation** is when a sound changes over time—like how your voice goes up when you're excited.

**Analogy:** Remember those LED candles that slowly fade in and out? That's modulation! In music, modulation adds movement and interest so sounds don't feel flat and boring.

### 📈 ADSR Envelope

This sounds scary but it's just a way to describe the **shape** of a sound:

- **A**ttack: How fast the sound starts (pop vs. slow swell)
- **D**ecay: How fast it drops to the sustain level  
- **S**ustain: How loud it stays while you hold the note
- **R**elease: How long it fades out after you let go

**Real-world analogy:** Think of a piano key vs. a violin. Hit a piano key and it hits you immediately (fast attack), then fades (decay/release). Bow a violin and it slowly swells in (slow attack), then holds while you bow (sustain). Different shapes, different feelings!

## Getting Started: Your First Project 🏁

### Inside Your Project

Your project has three tabs at the top:

## Upload: The Grand Entrance ☁️

### Adding Audio Files

Here's the deal: AudioForge needs audio to work with. Shocking, right?

**How to upload:**

1. Click the upload area or drag your file onto it
2. Supported formats: **MP3, WAV, FLAC** (and most common audio formats)
3. Watch the progress bar do its thing
4. Your file appears in the asset list! 🎉

### What Are "Assets"?

Everything in your project—original uploads AND AI-generated parts—is called an **asset**. Think of assets as your collection of audio building blocks.

### Working with Your Assets

Each asset has superpowers:

| Action | How | Why |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Preview** | Click the play button | Make sure it sounds right before committing |
| **Rename** | Click the pencil icon ✏️ | Give it a memorable name like "My Bass Line" |
| **Delete** | Click the trash icon 🗑️ | Remove something you don't need |
| **Detect BPM** | Click the music note 🎵 | Find the tempo of your audio |
| **Detect Key** | Click the music icon 🎼 | Find what musical key the audio is in |

### Sorting & Filtering

The asset list has controls to help you find what you need:

- **Filter buttons:** Show only Originals, only Stems, or Everything
- **Sort dropdown:** Newest, Oldest, Name, Duration
- **Search bar:** Type to find files by name

## Separate: The Magic Trick ✨

This is where things get *really* cool. The **Separate** tab uses AI to take a complete song and split it into individual tracks (stems).

### How Separation Works

1. Go to the **Separate** tab
2. Choose which uploaded file to process
3. Pick your separation mode:
   - **4 Stems** 🦵 - Vocals, Drums, Bass, and Other
   - **Vocals + Accompaniment** 🎤 - Just vocals vs. everything else
4. Choose your AI model (more on this below)
5. Click **Start Separation** and... wait. Magic takes time! ⏳

### Picking Your AI Model

Think of these like different chefs with different cooking styles:

| Model | Personality | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **HT Demucs** | The reliable all-rounder | Most songs—good balance of speed and quality |
| **HT Demucs FT** | The perfectionist | When you want more detail (takes longer) |
| **MDX** | The alternative chef | Different music styles, worth trying |
| **MDX Extra** | The detail-oriented | Maximum quality, patient people |

**Why care?** Different models work better for different songs! If one doesn't sound great, try another. 🎲

### After Separation

- Wait while the progress bar does its thing (this takes time—go stretch!)
- When done, switch to the **Mixer** tab
- Your stems appear as new assets you can now play with!

## Mixer: Where the Magic Happens 🎚️

Welcome to the control center! The **Mixer** is where you combine tracks and make things sound amazing.

> *"This is where the real fun begins. Put on your producer hat!"*

### The Playback Controls

At the top of the mixer:

| Control | What It Does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Play/Pause** | Start or stop playback |
| **Time Display** | Shows current position / total length |
| **Master Volume** | Controls overall loudness of everything |

### Individual Track Controls

Each track (stem) in the mixer has its own control panel:

```
[✓] [🔊] [🎧]  Track Name    [Vol: 70]  [Pan: 0]
```

Here's what each button does:

| Control | Icon | What It Does |
|---------|------|--------------|
| **Select** | ☑️ | Include/exclude this track from playback |
| **Mute** | 🔊/🔇 | Silence this track (click to toggle) |
| **Solo** | 🎧 | Only play THIS track, silence all others |
| **Volume** | V slider | Adjust loudness (0-100) |
| **Pan** | L/R slider | Move sound left or right (-100 to +100) |

### The Meters

The colored bars show you how loud each track is:

- 🟢 **Green** = Good level, plenty of headroom
- 🟡 **Amber** = Getting loud, pay attention
- 🔴 **Red** = Too loud! Turn it down or it'll sound distorted!

**Master Meter** shows the overall output with both **Peak** and **RMS** readings.

**Phase Meter** shows stereo correlation:
- **+100** = Perfect mono (same in both speakers)
- **-100** = Complete phase cancellation (sound might disappear!)
- **Around +50** = Good stereo image

### Timeline & Playhead

The waveform shows your audio visually:

- 🎵 **Waveform** = The shape of the sound
- 🔴 **Playhead** = The red line showing where you are
- 🖱️ **Click anywhere** on the timeline to jump to that spot!

### Quick Actions

Below the mixer:
- **Reset Mix** 🔄 - Put all sliders back to default
- **Unmute All** 🔊 - Turn all tracks back on  
- **Clear Solo** 🎧 - Stop soloing any track
